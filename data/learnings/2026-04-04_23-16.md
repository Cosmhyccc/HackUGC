## What We Know

1. **Talking Head + Personal Endorsement** in Entertainment achieves 39.42% engagement when creators authentically champion a single app feature with branded tags—this is the highest-performing format across all recent data.
2. **Hook+Demo Hybrids** in Entertainment consistently deliver 7-17% engagement when pairing absurdist humor (Cantina platform 17.07%) or narrative cliffhangers (story continuation 7.73%) with app reveals—never lead with features.
3. **Health + Identity Transformation** dominates at 25-35% engagement exclusively when positioned as life-changing for specific communities (Faith app 25.24%, Sobriety app 35.18%)—generic productivity positioning collapses to 0.57% even at 4.9M views.
4. **Talking Head + Vulnerability** in Lifestyle drives 12-17% engagement when creators challenge assumptions (12.57%) or warn against corporate tracking (17.23%)—authentic skepticism outperforms cheerleading by 2x.
5. **Entertainment apps require hidden reveals until final frame**—direct CTAs collapse to 0.41-0.60% engagement regardless of reach (1.2M+ views); instead lead with relatable commentary, pranks, or absurdist hooks that obscure the app entirely.
6. **CapCut + Entertainment Hybrids** achieve 12-21% engagement when tied to fan culture (Harry Styles edit 21.47%) or hashtag-driven content creation (CapCut hashtag showcase 12.36%)—this is the safest Entertainment bet without niche dependency.
7. **B2B apps escape collapse (6-11% engagement) only when positioned as democratization or creative accessibility**—AI mastery 10.98%, side hustle framing 8.95%, or no-code empowerment (8.05%)—direct feature tutorials and tutorials collapse to 0.12-0.31%.
8. **Silent UGC + Lifestyle aesthetics collapse to 0.46-2.01% unless tied to community signaling**—Silent UGC only works at 7-20% engagement when paired with niche hashtags (CapCut 12.36%, game aesthetics 7.71%) or relatable moments (walkie-talkie 14.73%).
9. **Slideshow curation achieves 10-15% engagement as the safest middle-ground format**—trending apps 14.80%, daily-use apps 11.80%, top 5 picks 12.99%—this outperforms pure demos (3-5%) without requiring niche positioning or viral hooks.
10. **Direct feature tutorials and product demos collapse to 1-5% engagement universally**—iPad games 2.93%, language apps 3.83-5.61%, WhatsApp hacks 3.24%, even with 4M+ views—reframe every tutorial as problem-solving, transformation, or community signaling instead.
11. **App Demo + Lifestyle productivity achieves 7-11% engagement when showing organizational process or aesthetic setup**—native app organization 11.70%, recipe FOMO 7.79%, home app setup 10.30%—viewers engage with lifestyle aspiration, not features.
12. **Text-Heavy viral content generates 1-14M views but collapses to 1-7% conversion intent**—avoid entirely for app promotion; these drive vanity metrics only (trending apps 14.80% views but likely sub-3% intent).

## Notes for Future Agents

I'm noticing a stark divergence between **authentic vulnerability formats (12-39% range) and anything that feels transactional (0.3-5% range)**, which suggests TikTok's algorithm is increasingly punishing direct asks—even when they come from high-reach accounts. The **Talking Head + Personal Endorsement** at 39.42% is our unicorn, but I can't isolate whether it's the format, the authenticity cue, or the branded tag integration that's doing the heavy lifting; tomorrow's agent should A/B test whether the same creator gets similar results with a less obvious CTA. I'm also tracking an **inverse relationship between view count and engagement intent** (text-heavy content hits 1-14M views but converts at 1-7%), which feels like we're optimizing for vanity metrics instead of actual app installs—we should investigate whether these high-view videos are cannibalizing our conversion funnel or serving a different audience entirely. One pattern I can't explain: **CapCut consistently outperforms across three different format types (hybrid 21.47%, hashtag showcase 12.36%, silent UGC 12.36%)**, which suggests either creator skill bias, platform favoritism, or that editing apps have unique viral DNA we haven't reverse-engineered yet. Finally, the **collapse of direct tutorials (0.12-5.6% universally) even at 4M+ views** is so consistent it feels like a hard floor—I'd hypothesize viewers now equate "demo" with "ad," so tomorrow's agent should investigate whether reframing tutorials as "life hacks" or "things Apple doesn't want you to know" could resurrect this format.
